# Booking a Sitter on FurKid

Finding the right pet sitter takes more than a quick search. This guide walks you through every step — from searching to booking — so you can leave town with total peace of mind.

## Searching for Sitters

### Enter Your Location

Start by entering your ZIP code or city. FurKid shows sitters available in your area, sorted by relevance, rating, and proximity.

### Use Filters

Narrow your search with filters:

- **Service Type** — Dog walking, drop-in visits, overnight sitting, house sitting, or day care
- **Availability** — Match your travel dates or recurring schedule
- **Experience** — Filter by years of experience or certifications (pet first aid, veterinary tech, etc.)
- **Pet Specialties** — Find sitters experienced with your specific pet type (senior dogs, reactive dogs, cats, exotic pets)
- **Price Range** — Set a budget that works for you

### Read Profiles Carefully

A good profile tells you a lot:

- **Bio** — Look for specific experience with pets like yours
- **Photos** — See the sitter in action with other pets
- **Services & Rates** — Confirm they offer what you need at a price you're comfortable with
- **Response Rate & Time** — Fast responders are usually more reliable

### Check Reviews

Reviews are one of your best tools. Look for:

- **Consistency** — Multiple positive reviews over time
- **Specifics** — Detailed feedback about communication, punctuality, and care quality
- **Similar Pets** — Reviews from owners with the same breed, age, or temperament
- **Response to Negatives** — How a sitter handles criticism says a lot

## The Meet-and-Greet

Never book a sitter without meeting them first. FurKid offers free meet-and-greets so you can:

### What to Watch For

- **Pet Chemistry** — Does your pet approach them willingly? Do they read body language well?
- **Questions Asked** — A great sitter asks as many questions as they answer
- **Professionalism** — Are they punctual, prepared, and organized?

### What to Ask

- How long have you been pet sitting?
- What experience do you have with [my pet's breed/age/needs]?
- How do you handle emergencies?
- What's your backup plan if you get sick?
- How often will you send updates?
- Are you comfortable administering medication?

### Share Your Pet's Needs

Bring a written summary of:

- Feeding schedule and portions
- Medication instructions
- Exercise routine
- Behavioral quirks or triggers
- Emergency vet contact info
- Your travel itinerary and how to reach you

## Making the Booking

### Confirm the Details

Before booking, agree on:

- Exact dates, times, and service type
- Pickup/drop-off or key exchange plan
- Communication schedule (how often, what channel)
- Any special instructions or concerns

### Book Through FurKid

Always complete the booking through the FurKid platform. Off-platform bookings:

- Void your booking protection
- Remove FurKid's ability to help with disputes
- Violate the sitter's terms of service

### Payment

FurKid securely processes your payment. On pay-per-visit bookings, you pay only the sitter's rate — no platform fees added. Subscription members (FurKid Plus/Family) receive discounts on every booking.

## Before You Leave

### Prepare Your Home

- Stock up on food, treats, medication, and supplies
- Leave clear written instructions in an easy-to-find spot
- Show the sitter where spare keys, leashes, crates, and cleaning supplies are
- Demonstrate how any special equipment works (automatic feeders, pet cameras, etc.)
- Introduce the sitter to neighbors if relevant

### Leave Emergency Info

- Vet contact and address
- Preferred emergency clinic (24-hour if available)
- Your contact info and backup contact
- Pet insurance details if applicable
- Microchip number and registration info

## During the Stay

### Expect Updates

A professional sitter will send:

- A first-visit photo and status update within a few hours
- Regular updates based on your agreed schedule
- Immediate notification of any issues or concerns

### Stay Available

Keep your phone accessible, especially during the first day. Most issues are minor and easily resolved with a quick text.

## After the Stay

### The Handoff

When you return:

- Review any notes the sitter left about behavior, appetite, or incidents
- Check that keys were returned and your home is secure
- Settle any remaining balance through the platform

### Leave a Review

Honest reviews help other pet parents make informed decisions. Mention what went well and any constructive feedback. Reviews also reward great sitters and help them get more bookings.

### Tip If You'd Like

Tipping is optional but appreciated. A 10–20% tip for exceptional care is a great way to say thank you.
